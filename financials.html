Contingency Plan:


In the Maximum Likelihood growth scenario, we would get a couple of loyal, primary clients. These would be high-end restaurants in Los Angeles. We would continue to supply to them and have a stable cash flow.

In a scenario where things got significantly better, we would see high growth. In this scenario, we would get a large client like LA Phil. This would lead us to other connections like other high-end music venues or other Philâ€™s in other states. We would see a high increase in our cash flow and increased brand awareness with our target consumer base.

In a scenario where things got worse, we would see a marked stagnancy in our growth. In this scenario, we would have minimal trials with clients, and they would dislike our product, failing to understand the difference between our product and other teas like Lipton or Teavana. In an even worse scenario, we would have trouble acquiring customers and communicating the value of our product.

Break-even Analysis


1. Minimum amount of cash needed to cover your costs:

In order for Folia tea to cover our costs of operation (BEQ), we need at least $2970.75 in cash in the bank.

2. The quantity of products you will have to sell per month to cover your costs (BEQ) including both fixed and variable costs:

100 units; per quarter total cost: ~ $2970.75

3. Variable costs:

Tea leaves - median value of tea 100g/3.5oz (~$25) x ~100 units = $2500

Delivery - $6.50/gallon x 20 gallons (1 gallon = ~20 miles) = $78

Packaging - $.98 (2 aluminum can) x 50 (~100 units) = $49

Teaware - $50 (per high-quality tea pot + spoon) x 5 (for each new customer) = $250

4. Fixed costs:

Vendor Liability Insurance - $31.25/month (average liability insurance) x 3 (months in a quarter) = $93.75
